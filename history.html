History of Japanese Curry Rice

Late 19th Century - Introduction to Japan

1850s-1860s

Japan opens its ports to Western trade after centuries of isolation during the Edo period.

1868

The Meiji Restoration begins, leading to significant modernization and Western influence.

1872

Curry is introduced to Japan by the British during the Meiji era. British naval officers bring curry recipes influenced by Indian cuisine to Japan.

Early 20th Century - Adoption and Adaptation

Early 1900s

Japanese military and navy begin to incorporate curry into their diet due to its nutritional value and ease of preparation. It is often served with rice.

1908

House Foods Corporation, one of the major food manufacturers in Japan, begins producing curry powder, making it more accessible to the general public.

Mid-20th Century - Popularization and Commercialization

1945-1952

Post-World War II, curry gains widespread popularity among Japanese households.

1950s

Instant curry roux is developed, making it easier for people to cook curry at home. Major companies like House Foods and S&B Foods start mass-producing curry roux.

Late 20th Century - Curry as a National Dish

1960s-1970s

Curry rice (kare raisu) becomes a staple in Japanese school lunches (kyushoku), further cementing its place in Japanese cuisine.

1983

The annual “Kanda Curry Grand Prix” starts in Tokyo, highlighting the cultural significance and variety of curry dishes in Japan.

1990s

Japanese curry restaurants begin to open internationally, spreading Japanese-style curry to other parts of the world.

21st Century - Modern Variations and Global Influence

2000s

Diverse variations of Japanese curry develop, including regional specialties like Hokkaido’s seafood curry and Okinawa’s taco rice curry.

2010s

Curry chains such as CoCo Ichibanya expand globally, bringing Japanese curry to a wider international audience.

2019

Japanese curry is featured in popular media, including anime and manga, further popularizing it among younger generations worldwide.

Present Day

Present Day

Japanese curry rice is enjoyed both domestically and internationally. It is known for its versatility, with various meat, seafood, and vegetable options, and is typically served with pickled vegetables like fukujinzuke or rakkyo.
